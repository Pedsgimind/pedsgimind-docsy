## 5) ðŸ  Home care and what helps (step-by-step)

> âœ… Most children can be safely treated at home if they stay hydrated.

### First 24â€“48 hours: hydration plan
> âœ… **Do this now:**
1) Use **oral rehydration solution** (ORS) if possible  
2) Give **small sips frequently** (every 1â€“2 minutes)  
3) If vomiting occurs, wait 5â€“10 minutes, then restart with smaller sips  
4) Track wet diapers/urine output

### How much to give (simple, practical)
- Aim for frequent small amounts rather than big cups.
- If your child vomits with larger amounts, reduce volume and increase frequency.

### What to feed
- Continue breast milk or formula for infants
- Offer bland foods as tolerated:
  - toast, rice, pasta, banana
  - soups, yogurt (if tolerated)
- Do not force food; hydration matters most.

### What usually makes it worse
- fruit juice, soda, sports drinks (too sugary)
- large, fatty meals early on

## 8) ðŸ©º How doctors diagnose it (what to expect)

### What the clinician will ask
- fluid intake and urine output
- vomiting/diarrhea frequency
- fever pattern
- exposure (daycare, travel)
- blood in stool

### Physical exam basics
- hydration check (mouth, tears, skin, pulse)
- belly exam

### Possible tests (and why)
- usually none
- stool test if blood in stool, severe illness, or prolonged diarrhea
- blood tests if dehydration is significant

### What tests are usually not needed
- imaging unless severe localized pain suggests something else

## 9) ðŸ§° Treatment options

### First-line treatment
- oral rehydration solution
- continued feeding as tolerated
- rest

### If not improving
- clinic assessment for dehydration
- anti-nausea medicine in selected cases (clinician-directed)

### Severe cases (hospital care)

#### Medication/treatment notes
- **What it does:** prevents dehydration, supports recovery  
- **Common side effects:** ORS may taste salty; vomiting may still occur early  
- **When to stop and seek help:** worsening dehydration, blood, green vomit, severe pain
